Origins of Alpha Mu

Modern day Kappa Sigma was founded one chilly evening in the fall of 1869, as five students attending the University of Virginia in Charlottesville gathered in the room of William Grigsby McCormick, at 46 East Lawn, and planted the seed of Brotherhood. For many weeks the bonds of friendship had drawn these five together; now the need became clear for a formal structure to contain their feelings. Thus, not only did the Founders formalize their friendship, but they also created a fraternity steeped in the traditions of the past and dedicated to the Pursuit of Learning. The new brothers recorded their bond in a Constitution and in an Oath that set forth the ideals and principles to all Kappa Sigmas today.

The Alpha Mu Chapter was established at the University of North Carolina in 1893. Its founding members were George Roscoe Little, Gerard Samuel Wittson, James Spenser Lewis, Thomas Pleasant Braswell, Jr., and Thomas Menan Hooker. The fraternity stressed fellowship, leadership, scholarship, and service. Today, we still hold these values dearly at UNC and will continue to do so in the future.

The Alpha Mu Chapter of Kappa Sigma at UNC exemplefies what defines a Kappa Sigma. We invite you to explore our webpage, and contact our chapter with any questions. Whether you are an Alumni, Brother, Parent, or Rush we welcome any questions about our UNC Kappa Sigma Chapter, you can find information regarding donations, rush, alumni meetings, and our academic information all throughout this site.
